Every year the Bay Area has been plagued by huge wildfires that affect the population in different scenarios, whether people loses their homes or get sick due the drastic changes on air quality. According to an article by The Mercury News, October is the most dangerous month historically for wildfire risk in California.

In this project, we will be creating visualizations using the Fire Department Calls for Service dataset to look at the months from September to November, over the past five years. We are going to be looking at fire trends and incidents that may correlate to the wildfires during those months, as well see the behaviour of them over the years.
